Push Notifications for Sign in Confirmation | Firefox Accounts | Mozilla

Aim of this project was to implement a form of 2-factor authentication in Firefox Accounts. Once completed, if a user has multiple devices connected to their Firefox Account, they will be able to receive a push notification to one of their other devices asking for confirmation when trying to log in to their Firefox Account. The project was completed in four parts:- Firefox Accounts verify login push notification for desktop: Modified the code of Mozilla Firefox (Desktop Client) to trigger a notification, using content from the payload on receiving a request from Firefox Accounts Auth Server. Sending request from the auth server: If the user is already signed in, this is checked in the database, the auth-server will send a push notification instead of an email for confirmation. Updated the confirm view to handle when verification is using push: The auth-server will determine whether to send a message via email or push. The confirm view was updated to display appropriate text depending on if the user should look in their email or at another device. Created a new view for sign in confirmation using push notification: Created a new view called confirmation view in fxa-content-server. As the user clicks on the push-notification for sign-in confirmation, the confirmation view appears.

Mentor: Sean McArthur, Senior Software Engineer, Mozilla, California

Study of protein adhesion on neural scaffolds

Biomaterials and Multiscale Mechanics Lab(BMML)
Department of Metallurgical and Materials Engineering, IIT Roorkee

The nervous system is one of the most important systems of the body and any damage to it can be lethal. Restoring the function of any damage in the nervous system is challenging due to its complexity and limited ability to regenerate. The nervous system consists of two main parts Central Nervous System (CNS) and Peripheral Nervous System (PNS). In the CNS, the formation of inhibitory glial scars and limited clearance of myelin makes the regeneration difficult.
The current existing treatments of PNS tissue regeneration are associated with certain drawbacks, therefore an extracellular matrix of scaffold is provided at the damaged site for the regeneration of tissues. Several studies are conducted for the enhancement of the properties of this scaffold. This Project aims to study the effects of protein adhesion on neural scaffold.

Mentor: Dr. Debrupa Lahiri, Assistant Professor, IIT Roorkee

Report: Study repory on affects of protein adhesion on neural scaffolds. (Report in Progress)

Study on student behaviour

Under the guidance of Dr. Jogendra Kumar Nayak
Department of Management Studies, IIT Roorkee

The purpose of this study was to observe the difference in behaviour of girl students when they study in single sex school and a co-education school. This research was conducted in three local schools of Roorkee. The academic achievement of girl students of different schools was calculated on the basis of CGPA alone, and there was very less difference in the same. One remarkable thing that came out of the study was that most of the girl students are satisfied with their schooling system be it CE or SS and also most of the girls prefer mixed environment after their schooling.

Mentor: Dr. Jogendra Kumar Nayak, Professor, Department of Management Studies, IIT Roorkee

Report: Behavioral changes in the girls of single sex schools and co-ed schools.
